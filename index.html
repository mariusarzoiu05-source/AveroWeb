Under the hood

Three layers of analysis.

All running locally. All in under a second.

1

Feature extraction

16 structural signals are pulled from the URL — length, subdomain count, special characters, TLD risk, entropy, and known suspicious patterns.

2

On-device ML model

A TFLite neural network, trained on 530,000 real phishing URLs via knowledge distillation, runs inference entirely locally — no internet needed.

3

Heuristic layer

A rule engine catches brand impersonation, IP-based hosts, redirect chains, and urgency tactics that pure ML may miss.

4

Clear verdict

Safe · Suspicious · Phishing · Critical. A numeric score plus a plain-language explanation of exactly why the decision was made.

Questions

Frequently
asked.

Does Avero upload my links or passwords?+

No. Every analysis runs entirely on your device using a local TFLite model. URLs, passwords, and images never reach any server. No account is required to scan.

How accurate is the detection?+

The ML model achieves ~87% accuracy, trained on 530,000 URLs. A heuristic layer adds extra coverage for patterns like brand impersonation and IP-based hosts.

Does it work without internet?+

Yes. Link scanning, password analysis, image OCR, and courses all work fully offline. Optional redirect-chain checks may use the network.

What Android version is required?+

Android 8.0 (API 26) and above — covering over 95% of active Android devices globally.

Is there an iOS version?+

Not yet. Avero is currently Android-only. Join the waitlist to be notified when iOS support arrives.

How does auto SMS protection work?+

Avero monitors incoming SMS and notifications from selected apps. When a URL is found, it is scanned locally and you're alerted immediately — no message content reaches any server.
